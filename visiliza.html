Bridging Worlds: STEM Perceptions in India & Ireland

A deep dive into how students and parents across India and Ireland view technology, coding, and STEM careers. This analysis uncovers key trends in confidence, interest, and the subtle cultural dynamics shaping the next generation of innovators.

The Confidence Gap: Use vs. Creation

Across both countries, students feel significantly more confident using computers for daily tasks than they do with the specific skill of coding. This highlights a global challenge: shifting students from being passive technology consumers to active creators.

The Interest-Confidence Paradox

While interest in Science, Technology, and Maths is relatively high, it doesn't automatically translate into coding confidence. This paradox suggests that sparking interest is only the first step; building practical skills through supportive, hands-on learning is crucial to empower students.
